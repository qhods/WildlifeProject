Bio

Orange-bellied Parrots can be found in Australia more specifically the southwest area of Tasmania. These birds are only about 46 grams or 1.6 ounces. Their size is 20 centimeters long, in conclusion these birds are very small animals. That does not stop them from being seen. The males are very bright birds consisting of a bright green body with accents of yellow green mix on their chest, blue stripe on its head as well as blue wings, and an orange patch at the bottom of their belly. Female birds have the same look but slightly darker colors. Orange Bellied Parrots will have one breeding partner for life, the only time they will find another is if one of the partners dies. Breeding happens from November to February, this is when the females create nests in hollowed out Smithton peppermint trees to incubate the eggs. Like most birds they will eat berries, seeds, and beaded or shrubby glasswort.

Population Decrease

The population of these critically endangered birds are 14 known birds left in the wild. Other factors that contribute to their decline in population is due to:

  1. habitat loss
  2. being preyed on by cats and foxes
  3. spread of harmful weeds
  4. flying into structures
  5. inbreeding since there is such a small population

What can be done?

The Australian Government has been working tirelessly along with other organizations to protect the Orange-Bellied Parrots. There has been a promised $3.2 million in efforts to bring back this species. Captive breeding has been a big success in bringing these birds back to a decent population size. The hope is that there will be enough captive breeding so that eventually more can be released back into the wild. The Orange-Bellied Parrots breed in the summer in Tasmania which is off the coast of Australia and breed in the winter on the coast of South Australia. Since they breed in two different places there has been an effort to conserve their habitat in both areas as well as improving it. There has been an attempt to control the animals that prey on these birds to give them a chance to increase their population.
